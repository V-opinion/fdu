The Power of National Symbols in Modern Societies

National flags have always embodied the values, traditions, and beliefs of their countries. Historically, many flags featured religious symbols—crosses, crescents, stars—that reflected the state’s dominant faith. But today, societies are more diverse than ever. As Europe continues the journey to separate church from state and religion from politics, these traditional symbols often feel outdated and divisive..

The Case for Inclusive Flag Design

An inclusive flag design embraces every citizen, regardless of background or belief. This shift reflects a modern, pluralistic society. It means moving beyond religious imagery and adopting symbols that speak to unity, citizenship, and digital-age identity. For example, flags could incorporate country domain extensions (.se, .no, .dk, .fi), making them instantly recognizable and accessible for all.

The Emergence of a New Flag Design Industry


Why do national flags with religious or difficult-to-interpret symbols still fly above the headquarters of the UN, EU, and NATO?
These symbols—often inherited from historical eras—reflect identities that exclude or confuse
modern citizens in today’s pluralistic world. As Europe moves toward fuller separation between
religion and politics, the time has come to redesign the symbols that represent us all.
A new flag design industry is emerging—one that reimagines national identity through symbols of unity, equality, and digital citizenship. This transformation goes beyond aesthetics. It is a cultural and democratic reform that invites every citizen, regardless of belief, to feel represented.

From History to Harmony: Learning Through Design


Imagine a preschool classroom where children learn the alphabet by looking at flags. Instead of
seeing religious crosses on Nordic flags, they see letters formed by their national domain symbols: .se for Sweden, .no for Norway, .dk for Denmark, .fi for Finland. These flags teach more than colors and shapes—they teach language, identity, and shared belonging.
Replacing historical religious symbols with civic or digital ones empowers children to connect with
their nation through inclusion, not exclusion. It also harmonizes national branding with the digital
age, where the country’s domain name already carries symbolic power worldwide.

A New Industry With Social Impact


This vision is not just cultural—it’s economic. The new flag design industry can generate skilled
jobs in design, manufacturing, education, and digital marketing. It opens opportunities for
collaboration between artists, educators, and policy innovators, strengthening Europe’s creative
economy while reinforcing democratic values.
We have already presented an EU citizens' initiative to promote this new field, aiming to inspire

Completing the Separation Between Church and State


The movement for new flag design connects directly to the unfinished task of separating church and
state, and religion from politics. In Sweden, this issue has resurfaced with renewed urgency
following the secret election of September 21, 2025. Ensuring that national symbols reflect the
entire population, rather than any single faith, is essential to completing this democratic evolution.
Our mission is clear: to promote designs, policies, and civic reforms that build a world where
everyone—regardless of faith or background—can stand united under a shared, inclusive flag.
